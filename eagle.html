Eagle

Eagles are majestic birds of prey known for their impressive size, powerful beaks, and keen eyesight. Belonging to the family Accipitridae, eagles are found on every continent except Antarctica, with different species adapted to various habitats ranging from forests and mountains to grasslands and wetlands. With wingspans that can exceed two meters in some species, eagles are skilled hunters capable of catching a wide range of prey, including fish, mammals, and birds. They are apex predators in their ecosystems and play a vital role in maintaining ecological balance. Eagles are also revered in many cultures and symbolize strength, freedom, and power. Due to habitat loss, pollution, and other human-related threats, some eagle species are facing conservation challenges, making conservation efforts essential to ensure their survival for future generations to admire and appreciate their beauty and significance in nature.
